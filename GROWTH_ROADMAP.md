# Church's Cookout — Growth Roadmap
### From Zero to 1 Sponsorship/Month + Side Income

---

## The Opportunity

Your niche — Indian-inspired cooking, vegan-friendly, modern twist — is genuinely strong for brand partnerships. You're not competing in the oversaturated "generic food blogger" space. You sit at the intersection of three growing audiences: the South Asian diaspora cooking for themselves, the mainstream audience discovering Indian cuisine, and vegans/vegetarians looking for meals that are actually exciting. That specificity is what sponsors pay for.

Realistic timeline to 1 sponsor/month: **9–12 months** if you execute consistently. Here's the exact playbook.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1–2)

**Goal: Content library + channel setup. Zero growth expectations yet.**

### Platforms to Launch (in priority order)

1. **Instagram** — Primary platform. Food is Instagram's strongest vertical. Reels drive discovery, grid posts build authority. Post here first, repurpose everywhere else.
2. **TikTok** — Highest organic reach for new accounts. Algorithm favors new creators more than any other platform. Essential for growing fast with zero followers.
3. **YouTube** — Slower burn but highest monetization value. Start with Shorts (repurposed from Reels/TikTok) to build presence before investing in long-form.

**Do NOT try to be everywhere at once.** Master Instagram + TikTok first. Add YouTube long-form at Month 3+.

### Username / Handle
Use **@churchscookout** consistently across all platforms. Brand coherence matters.

### What to Build Before You Post a Single Thing
- [ ] 9 pieces of content shot and edited (a "content cushion") so you never post under pressure
- [ ] A consistent visual style — your warm amber/earth tone palette from the website translates well to video
- [ ] A 15-second "this is what I make" intro clip you can use as your bio link video
- [ ] Google/Mailchimp account set up for the newsletter (this is your most valuable asset long-term)

### Content Pillars (stick to these)
1. **The Recipe** — the core content. Full cook, hero shot at the end.
2. **The Technique** — one specific technique that makes your food better (e.g., "why I bloom spices in hot oil")
3. **The Story** — the cultural/personal context behind a dish. Your backstory with your parents' Indian roots + cooking for your vegan wife is genuinely compelling content.
4. **The Ingredient Spotlight** — kala namak, ajwain, kasuri methi — ingredients your audience has never heard of. High engagement because it's educational.

## Phase 2: Consistency & Growth (Months 2–6)

**Goal: 5k Instagram followers, 3k TikTok followers, email list of 500+**

### Posting Schedule (non-negotiable)
| Platform | Frequency | Format |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Instagram Reels | 4x/week | 30–60 sec recipe |
| Instagram Grid | 2x/week | Hero food photo + recipe teaser |
| TikTok | 4x/week | Same content as Reels, re-captioned |
| YouTube Shorts | 2x/week | Repurposed from Reels |
| YouTube Long-form | 1x/week | Full recipe video (start at Month 3) |

**Batch filming is the only sustainable way to do this.** Dedicate 1 day every 2 weeks to film 8–10 pieces of content at once.

### What Actually Drives Growth at This Stage
- **Hooks in the first 2 seconds** — every video must start with a result or a provocative statement. "This is the chicken burger I make when I need to impress someone" beats "Hi guys, today I'm making..."
- **Trending audio on Reels/TikTok** — food content + trending sound = algorithm boost. Spend 10 min/week identifying trending sounds to use.
- **Comment engagement** — reply to every comment in the first hour after posting. This signals engagement to the algorithm.
- **Collab with other creators** — find 2–3 Indian food creators or vegan creators at similar follower counts and do recipe collab posts. Cross-promotion is the fastest growth lever.

### Email List Building
- Add a newsletter signup to your website (done in this update)
- Offer a "free meal plan" as the lead magnet — e.g., "7-Day Indian-Inspired Vegan Meal Plan" as a PDF download
- Use **Mailchimp** (free up to 500 contacts) or **Kit (formerly ConvertKit)** for creators
- Send 1 email per week: the recipe you posted + one thing people didn't see on social

## Phase 3: Monetization Begins (Months 4–8)

**Goal: First paid partnerships, multiple income streams running**

### When You're Ready to Pitch Sponsors
You don't need 100k followers. Micro-influencers (5k–50k with high engagement) regularly command $150–$800 per post. The key metric sponsors actually care about: **engagement rate** (likes + comments ÷ followers). Target: 4%+. At 5k followers with 4% engagement, that's 200 interactions per post — enough to land micro-deals.

### Target Brand Categories (ranked by match to your content)
1. **Indian grocery / spice brands** — MDH, Everest, Shan, Deep Foods, Swad. Perfect fit, often sponsor smaller creators.
2. **Cookware** — Made In, Zwilling, Lodge Cast Iron. Food creators are their primary influencer channel.
3. **Vegan food brands** — Miyoko's, Kite Hill, Forager. Growing budgets, love niche creators.
4. **Specialty grocery** — Whole Foods (regional), Thrive Market, Indian grocery apps like Asha Foods or iShopIndian.
5. **Kitchen appliances** — KitchenAid (you literally use it in your recipes), Instant Pot.
6. **Meal kit / grocery delivery** — HelloFresh, Misfits Market, Imperfect Foods.

### How to Land the First Sponsorship (Cold Outreach Playbook)

**Step 1: Build your media kit** (the Work With Me page on your site does this — see website updates)

**Step 2: Find the right contact**
- Search LinkedIn: "[Brand Name] influencer marketing" or "brand partnerships manager"
- Or email: partnerships@[brand].com, creators@[brand].com
- Many Indian brands don't have formal programs yet — a direct DM on Instagram to their brand account often works

**Step 3: The pitch email**
Subject: `Partnership Opportunity — Indian-Inspired Food Creator @ Church's Cookout`

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm Churchill, the creator behind Church's Cookout — an Indian-inspired cooking platform focused on modern, vegan-friendly takes on South Asian cuisine. My audience skews [demographic] and engages heavily around [specific content type].
>
> I've been featuring [Brand Product] organically in my content — [specific example] — and thought a formal partnership could be a great fit. I reach [X] followers across Instagram and TikTok, with an average engagement rate of [X]%.
>
> I'd love to share my media kit and talk through what a collaboration could look like. Would you have 15 minutes this week?
>
> [Your name]

**Step 4: Pricing**
Start lower to build case studies, then raise rates:
- Months 4–6: $100–$300/post (to get the first deals, build testimonials)
- Months 7–12: $300–$600/post
- Year 2+: $600–$1,500/post once you have proven engagement data

Aim for **3-post packages** rather than one-offs — more predictable income, better ROI pitch to brands.

## Income Streams (Multiple, Parallel)

Here are all the revenue levers ranked by effort-to-return:

### 1. Brand Sponsorships (Primary Goal)
Already covered above. Target: $300–$600/deal × 1/month = **$300–$600/month** by Month 9.

### 2. Affiliate Marketing (Passive, Start Now)
Add affiliate links to ingredients and tools you already use in your recipes.
- **Amazon Associates** — add links to every specialty ingredient (kala namak, kasuri methi, tandoori masala). 1–3% commission but passive.
- **ShareASale** / **Impact** — higher rates, more brand options (cookware, specialty food)
- **LTK (Like To Know It)** — popular with food creators for linking exact products used

Add an "Shop This Recipe" section to recipe pages. Even $50–$150/month from passive affiliate income is worth the 2-hour setup.

### 3. YouTube AdSense
Start earning at 1,000 subs + 4,000 watch hours (typically Month 6–9). Food content CPM: $3–$8 per 1,000 views. At 10k views/month: **$30–$80/month**. Not transformative but grows with the channel.

### 4. Digital Products (Month 6+)
Your highest-margin revenue source once you have an audience.
- **Meal plan PDFs** — "4-Week Indian Vegan Meal Plan" — $15–$25 each. Sell via Gumroad or your website.
- **Recipe ebook** — "Church's Cookout: 30 Indian-Inspired Recipes" — $12–$20. This is also a sponsorship asset (brands co-brand on digital products).
- Target: 20 sales/month at $15 = **$300/month**

### 5. Newsletter Sponsorships (Month 9+)
Once your email list hits 1,500+, you can sell a newsletter ad slot for $50–$150/issue. Weekly newsletter = $200–$600/month from a single sponsor placement. Newsletter sponsors are different from social sponsors — often smaller, more niche brands (Indian grocery apps, specialty food companies).

## Video Content Strategy

### Short-Form (Instagram Reels / TikTok) — Your Primary Growth Engine

**The 3-part formula that works:**
1. **Hook (0–2 sec):** Show the finished dish + one bold statement. "This tandoori marinade took me 5 years to get right."
2. **Middle (3–45 sec):** The actual recipe, fast-cut, visual-first. No talking head unless you're storytelling.
3. **CTA (last 3 sec):** "Full recipe on churchscookout.com" or "Save this for your next dinner party."

**Video formats to rotate:**
- The 60-second recipe
- The "one technique that changes everything" (e.g., blooming spices in hot oil)
- "POV: cooking for my vegan wife" — personal/relatable story format
- Ingredient education ("What is kala namak and why does it make tandoori taste like a restaurant?")
- "Guess what's in this" — mystery ingredient reveal
- Before/after (basic version vs. your version of a dish)

**Equipment to start:** iPhone + good window light + a $30 ring light for when you shoot at night. Don't wait for a camera upgrade.

### Long-Form YouTube (Start Month 3)

**Video format:**
- 8–15 minute recipe videos with story context
- Title formula: `[Dish Name] — [Compelling Hook]` e.g., "Paneer Tikka From Scratch — The 2-Marinade Technique That Changes Everything"
- Always tell the cultural story — why this dish, where it comes from, your family connection. This is the differentiator that turns viewers into subscribers.

## The One Thing That Kills Most Food Creators

**Inconsistency.** The algorithm punishes gaps. Missing a week because you're busy is fine occasionally. Missing 3 weeks in a row and you lose your momentum and have to rebuild. The batch filming system (1 filming day every 2 weeks) is your protection against this.

Set a 6-month calendar block now. Treat filming days like a standing meeting you can't cancel.
